AT A GLANCE \\

This carpool web application is designed with my specific college fellowship in mind. Many students do not have their own means of transportation for attending church, so this application facilitates the process of signing up for rides. There is also a short puzzle to entertain people after the carpool form.

Check it out here!

DESIGN GOALS \\

The initial goal was to improve upon the old system, which involved making a Facebook post every week where people would comment for rides. This had its drawbacks in that it was tedious for the carpool admiral to organize, and it limited church attendance to people who had a Facebook account.

SCREENS \\

The landing screen introduces users to the carpool system, which involves a weekly form that they can fill out to indicate whether they need a ride to fellowship that week. After the form, users can attempt a short puzzle created by myself purely for fun.

The form is constructed specifically to match the system that is currently in place. Users must input basic information such as their name, email address, and which service they need a ride to. There are also other options for going directly home after service or going out for food.

On submission, users are notified with a pop-up. The submitted information is then loaded directly into a Google spreadsheet, which is organized so that I can easily group passengers who live in the same location together to facilitate pickups.

Finally, users have the option of attempting a short puzzle that I create every week. There is no consequence for not completing it, but it simply exists as something fun for people to attempt if they choose to.

USER EXPERIENCE \\

After initially deploying this webapp in the early summer, I sent out a Google form to everyone in the fellowship who had used the website asking for their constructive input on each aspect of the website. With this, I made two major changes:

  • I implemented a confirmation email system so people would know if they had already signed up for a ride.

  • I added a FAQ page to help clear up confusions for newcomers.
