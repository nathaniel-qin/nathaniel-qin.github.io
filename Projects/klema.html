AT A GLANCE \\

Klema.io is a social media network that aims to better connect Christian missionaries with their supporters. The website provides functionalities such as writing blog posts, sharing prayer requests, and creating and joining communities.

Check it out here!

AFFILIATION \\ THE CULTIVATION PROJECT

TCP is a non-profit organization founded by Cameron Kuey comprised of filmmakers, photographers, developers, and artists whose goal is to connect missionaries, churches, and ordinary people. I was able to join the TCP team as a volunteer web developer intern for the summer of 2018.

DESIGN GOALS \\

The main issue that klema.io seeks to combat is the difficulty that missionaries face when connecting with their supporters. Currently, many missionaries must juggle a variety of different platforms, not only for providing updates to supporters (e-mail, social media, phone calls, etc.) but also for handling donations (cash, check, online payment platforms, etc.).

SCREENS \\

The activity feed serves as the home page for klema.io. It shows users recent blog posts by other users that they follow. Prayer requests are also displayed along the right side, sorted by recency.

Each user has a personal profile as well, which includes a photo, and personal information including a description, experience, etc. From this page, users can view recent prayer requests as well as recent posts.

One of klema.io's main functionalities is the ability to write and read blog posts. Users can view and like blog posts written by others.

Klema helps people of the same church, organization, or missions team find each other and stay connected through communities. The community page provides a description of the community, and also displays posts and prayer requests by its members.

Users have their own personal settings which can be accessed via the navbar dropdown. Interactive notifications help notify the user of any changes that have been made to their settings.

USER EXPERIENCE \\

While I was not quite involved with the UX design of this website, I made sure to implement common user-friendly designs, such as browser reactivity, settings notifications, and more to do my best in improving the overall user experience.
